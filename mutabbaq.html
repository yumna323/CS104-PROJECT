    • Ingredients:

    For the Dough:
    2 cups all-purpose flour
    ½ teaspoon salt
    2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    ½ cup water (adjust as needed)

    For the Filling:
    250g ground meat (beef or lamb)
    1 onion, finely chopped
    2 garlic cloves, minced
    1 tomato, finely chopped
    2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
    1 tablespoon curry powder
    1 teaspoon ground cumin
    1 teaspoon ground coriander
    Salt and pepper to taste
    1 egg (beaten, for sealing and binding)

    Steps:

    1. Prepare the Dough:
    In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and vegetable oil. Gradually add water and knead until a soft dough forms.
    Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
    2. Prepare the Filling:
    Heat a pan over medium heat and cook the ground meat until browned.
    Add onion, garlic, tomato, chilies, and spices. Cook until the mixture is dry and well-combined.
    Remove from heat and let it cool. Mix in the beaten egg to bind the filling.
    3. Shape the Mutabbaq:
    Divide the dough into small portions and roll each into a thin square or rectangle.
    Place a spoonful of filling in the center and fold the edges over to form a sealed parcel.
    4. Cook the Mutabbaq:
    Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil.
    Cook the parcels until golden brown and crispy on both sides, about 3–5 minutes per side.
    5. Serve:
    Serve warm with yogurt, chutney, or a simple salad.
